Daily incident distribution (30-day window)

Each dot = one server's non-compliance incident. X-axis = the date it became non-compliant. Y-axis = hours to resolve. Blue = resolved, red = still ongoing (counted from first_seen to now). Why scatter and not bar: on days with multiple incidents, a stacked bar would sum hours together, but that sum isn't a real metric (two 10-hr incidents stacked to 20h means nothing). Each dot keeps every incident identifiable. Hover for details.

MTTR by team & OS (30-day window)

Median resolution hours per team, split by OS. Lower is better. Bars over the dashed 72h line are missing SLA.

Resolution time distribution (30-day window)

How many incidents fall in each duration bucket. Tells you whether the problem is volume (many quick ones) or long-tail (a few that linger forever).

Weekly MTTR trend (last 12 weeks)

Median + P90 hours-to-resolve, by week. Median tracks typical experience; P90 tracks worst-case. Watch for drift independent of the 30-day window.

SLA compliance trend (30-day window)

Weekly % of incidents resolved within 72 hours.

Currently ongoing — aging (live, all dates)

Top 15 oldest unresolved incidents. The "what's on fire right now" view. Bars past the 72h marker are already in SLA breach.
